Utah Heat Pump Rebates & Tax Credits: The Complete 2026 Guide

Most Utah homeowners know about one rebate program. There are three — and they stack. Rocky Mountain Power Wattsmart rebates (up to $3,000 for cold-climate units), Dominion Energy ThermWise incentives for dual-fuel systems, and the federal IRA Section 25C tax credit (up to $2,000) can be combined to cut a typical $8,000 installation to roughly $3,100. This guide covers every program, eligibility requirements, and the step-by-step application process.

Do Heat Pumps Work in Utah's Cold Winters?

The short answer is yes — if you choose the right equipment. Modern cold-climate heat pumps maintain full heating capacity to 5°F and continue operating down to -13°F, which addresses the performance gap that gave older heat pumps a bad reputation in cold climates. This guide covers how heat pump performance varies across Utah's climate zones (Salt Lake Valley vs. Davis County vs. Morgan County), when a dual-fuel system makes more sense, and a real energy cost comparison using Utah utility rates.

The Complete Guide to VRF Systems for Utah Commercial Buildings

Variable Refrigerant Flow systems deliver 30–40% better efficiency than traditional rooftop units, simultaneous heating and cooling in different zones, and near-silent indoor operation. This comprehensive guide covers how VRF works, how Daikin VRV performs in Utah's cold winters, which building types benefit most (medical offices, mixed-use, hotels, historic structures), which buildings it does not suit, installation timeline, and ROI analysis using Utah energy costs.
