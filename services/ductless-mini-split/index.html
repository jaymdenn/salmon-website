Ductless Mini-Split Installation in Northern Utah

Flexible, efficient heating and cooling for rooms that your central system can't reach — from home additions and bonus rooms to converted garages and historic homes without ductwork.

A ductless mini-split system delivers heating and cooling directly to specific rooms without requiring ductwork. The outdoor unit connects to one or more indoor air handlers through a small hole in the wall — typically 3 inches in diameter — carrying refrigerant, power, and communication lines. The result is precise, independent comfort control for any space, at efficiencies that often exceed traditional ducted systems.

In Utah, mini-splits solve problems that central HVAC simply can't address economically: the sunroom addition that bakes in July, the bonus room over the garage that's always cold, the basement bedroom the central system barely reaches, or the detached shop that needs year-round comfort. Salmon HVAC has been installing ductless systems across Davis and Weber County for years and can recommend the right configuration for virtually any residential or light commercial application.

When a Ductless Mini-Split Is the Right Solution

  • Home additions and new construction without duct runs — Adding ductwork to reach a new room often costs more than the mini-split itself, involves opening up finished walls and ceilings, and creates permanent modifications to your home. A mini-split serves the same space in a day with minimal disruption.
  • Older Utah homes with no ductwork — Many homes in Bountiful, Centerville, and South Salt Lake were built with boiler or radiator heat and no forced-air duct system. Adding a mini-split is far more practical than retrofitting an entire duct system.
  • Garages, workshops, and ADUs — A detached garage workshop or accessory dwelling unit benefits enormously from independent climate control. Mini-splits heat and cool these spaces efficiently without connecting to the home's main system.
  • Supplement to existing HVAC with comfort problems — If your master bedroom or home office is always warmer or cooler than the rest of the house, a mini-split for that room solves the problem without modifying your existing duct system.
  • Server rooms and home offices requiring precise temperature control — A mini-split maintains consistent temperatures independent of the rest of the home — exactly what electronics and home offices need.
  • Energy savings in partially occupied homes — With a multi-zone mini-split, you can condition only the rooms you're using, not the entire house. This is particularly valuable in Utah's shoulder seasons when you only need cooling in a few rooms.

Single-Zone vs. Multi-Zone Mini-Split Systems

A single-zone system consists of one outdoor unit connected to one indoor air handler. These are straightforward and cost-effective for a single room or defined space. Capacities range from 9,000 BTU (small bedroom) to 36,000 BTU (large open-concept space).

A multi-zone system uses one outdoor unit connected to two, three, or four indoor air handlers, each serving a separate room or zone. Each indoor head operates independently — one room can be cooling while another is heating, or one can be off while another runs. Multi-zone systems are more cost-effective than installing separate single-zone systems for each room, as you share the outdoor unit and reduce overall installation cost.

We help you determine which configuration makes the most sense based on your specific spaces, usage patterns, and budget.

Our Mini-Split Installation Process

  1. Site evaluation and system selection — We evaluate the space, determine the appropriate capacity, and recommend the best indoor unit type (wall-mounted, ceiling cassette, floor console, or concealed duct) for the application.
  2. Indoor unit placement — We choose the mounting location for optimal airflow distribution and minimal visual impact, taking into account wall structure, furniture placement, and your aesthetic preferences.
  3. Line set and electrical routing — We run the refrigerant lines, communication wires, and condensate drain through a small penetration in the wall, using line hide covers on exterior surfaces for a clean appearance.
  4. Outdoor unit installation — We mount the outdoor unit on a pad or wall bracket with proper clearances for airflow and maintenance access, making all refrigerant and electrical connections.
  5. System commissioning — We pressure-test the refrigerant circuit, evacuate the line set, release refrigerant from the outdoor unit, and verify all operating parameters before handing you the remote.
  6. Remote and app configuration — Most Daikin mini-splits are Wi-Fi enabled. We configure the app on your phone and walk you through all operating modes: cooling, heating, dry, fan-only, and auto.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does mini-split installation cost in Utah?

A single-zone ductless mini-split system typically costs $2,000–$5,000 installed in Northern Utah, depending on the capacity, brand, efficiency rating, and installation complexity. Multi-zone systems that serve 2–4 rooms from one outdoor unit run $5,000–$12,000 or more. All estimates are free with no obligation.

Do mini-splits work for heating in Utah winters?

Yes. Cold-climate mini-split models, including those in Daikin's lineup, are rated to heat effectively down to -13°F. For a typical Wasatch Front winter, a properly sized cold-climate mini-split handles heating without backup in most locations. In areas with more extreme cold or at higher elevations, pairing with a backup heat source is worth considering.

Can I add a mini-split to a home that already has central ductwork?

Absolutely — this is one of the most common applications. Mini-splits excel at supplementing existing systems in rooms the central HVAC can't reach well: sunrooms, home additions, converted garages, bonus rooms over garages, or any space added after the original duct system was designed. You get independent temperature control in those spaces without tearing into walls.

How many zones do I need for a multi-zone mini-split?

One zone per room or area you want to control independently. A common setup might be: master bedroom as one zone, home office as another, and a sunroom as a third — all served by a single outdoor unit. Each indoor head has its own remote or app control, so each space maintains its own temperature independently regardless of what the other zones are doing.
