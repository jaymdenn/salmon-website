AC Maintenance & Tune-Up Services in Northern Utah

One annual visit keeps your cooling system running efficiently all summer — and prevents the mid-July breakdowns that always seem to happen at the worst possible time.

Utah's climate is hard on air conditioning equipment. Months of intense UV radiation degrade belts and plastic components. Blowing cottonwood seeds and dust clog condenser coils. Alkaline water deposits build up on coil surfaces. Then after a long winter sitting idle, you flip the AC on in May and hope it works. Annual maintenance by a qualified technician is the single most effective thing you can do to extend the life of your cooling system and avoid an emergency repair call on the hottest week of the year.

Salmon HVAC technicians perform detailed, documented tune-ups — not a 30-minute checkup and a signature. We inspect, clean, measure, and test every major component and give you a written report of what we found.

Signs Your AC Needs Maintenance (Or Is Overdue)

  • It's been more than 12 months since your last service — If you can't remember when a technician last looked at your system, it's time. Annual service is the minimum; twice a year (spring and fall) is ideal for combination heat pump systems.
  • Higher-than-usual electricity bills — A dirty condenser coil is one of the most common causes of efficiency loss. It's also one of the easiest fixes. If your Rocky Mountain Power bill has climbed without a change in usage habits, your coils likely need cleaning.
  • The outdoor unit is visibly dirty or packed with debris — Grass clippings, cottonwood fluff, pet hair, and dust all collect in the condenser fins. This restricts airflow and forces the system to work harder.
  • Longer run times to cool the house — When a system that used to cool your home in 20 minutes now takes 45, efficiency has dropped. Coil cleaning and refrigerant checks often restore normal performance.
  • Musty smell when the AC runs — This usually indicates mold or bacteria growing on the evaporator coil or in the condensate drain pan. It's a hygiene issue as much as a mechanical one, and it's addressable.
  • Visible ice on the outdoor unit or refrigerant lines — Ice is a sign of restricted airflow or low refrigerant — both preventable with annual service.

What's Included in Our AC Tune-Up

Here's exactly what a Salmon HVAC tune-up covers — we leave nothing to chance:

  1. Condenser coil cleaning — We use a coil cleaner and rinse the outdoor condenser coil to remove accumulated dirt, debris, and oxidation. This alone can restore 10–20% efficiency in a neglected unit.
  2. Evaporator coil inspection — We inspect the indoor evaporator coil for dirt buildup and biological growth. If cleaning is needed, we include it.
  3. Refrigerant pressure check — We connect digital manifold gauges and verify that refrigerant charge is within manufacturer specification. Low refrigerant indicates a leak that needs to be located and repaired.
  4. Electrical component inspection — We test capacitors with a capacitance meter (a capacitor showing 10% or more degradation gets flagged), check contactor condition, inspect wiring for heat damage, and verify that amperage draw is within spec.
  5. Condensate drain flush — We pour a treatment solution through the drain to prevent algae buildup that can clog the line and cause water backup into the air handler.
  6. Thermostat calibration check — We verify the thermostat is reading temperature accurately and staging the system correctly.
  7. Airflow and temperature differential measurement — We measure supply and return air temperatures to verify the system is achieving the expected 18–22°F temperature split across the coil.
  8. Written service report — You receive a documented summary of every item checked, its condition, and any recommendations — with estimated costs so you can plan ahead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my AC serviced?

Once a year, ideally in spring before the cooling season begins. Utah's dusty environment and long, hard summers mean your system accumulates more debris than in milder climates. Annual maintenance keeps the system running efficiently and catches small problems before they become expensive ones in the middle of July.

What's included in an AC tune-up?

A thorough AC tune-up includes: cleaning condenser and evaporator coils, checking refrigerant charge, inspecting and tightening electrical connections, testing capacitors and contactors, lubricating moving parts, clearing condensate drain lines, checking thermostat calibration, and measuring airflow and temperature differential. We provide a written report of everything found.

Does annual AC maintenance really save money?

Yes, consistently. A dirty condenser coil alone can reduce efficiency by 20–30%, meaning your system runs longer to achieve the same cooling and you pay more on your Rocky Mountain Power bill. Regular maintenance also extends equipment life by 3–5 years on average. The tune-up typically pays for itself in energy savings within the first summer.

When is the best time to schedule an AC tune-up in Utah?

Late March through May is ideal — after the last cold snap and before temperatures climb into the 80s and 90s. Scheduling in spring means we catch any issues before you need the system, and our schedule is more flexible than in peak summer when emergency calls fill our days. That said, we perform tune-ups year-round.
